About Noxolo Duma

A Clinician, Researcher & Advocate for Better Health

Noxolo Duma is a registered physiotherapist and PhD Candidate at the University of KwaZulu-Natal (UKZN), where she focuses her doctoral research on developing contextualised, evidence-based rehabilitation models for children with Cerebral Palsy in South Africa.

Holding a Master of Public Health, Noxolo bridges the gap between clinical physiotherapy practice and public health — bringing a uniquely broad perspective to patient care and community-based rehabilitation.

Who We Are

Clinically Excellent. Research-Driven. People-Centred.

Noxolo Duma Physiotherapy is founded and led by Noxolo E. Duma — a registered physiotherapist, Master of Public Health graduate, and current PhD Candidate at the University of KwaZulu-Natal (UKZN).

Noxolo's doctoral research focuses on developing a Contextualised Evidence-Based Interprofessional Rehabilitation Model Responsive to the Needs of Children with Cerebral Palsy in KwaZulu-Natal — research that directly informs the quality of care provided at this practice.

With expertise spanning paediatric rehabilitation, neurological conditions, public health, mental health, and community-based rehabilitation, this practice offers a uniquely comprehensive and evidence-informed approach to physiotherapy care.

What We Offer

Our Physiotherapy Services

Comprehensive, specialist physiotherapy grounded in research — from paediatric rehab to neurological recovery and beyond.

👶

Paediatric Rehabilitation

Expert physiotherapy for children with Cerebral Palsy, developmental delays, and paediatric neurological conditions. Informed by Noxolo Duma's own published research on CP management in low- and middle-income countries.

🧠

Neurological Rehabilitation

Assessment and treatment of neurological conditions affecting movement, balance, and functional independence. Evidence-based approaches for stroke, brain injury, Parkinson's, and more.

Manual Therapy

Preventive Medicine & Health Promotion

Proactive physiotherapy programmes to prevent injury and disability before they occur — personalised to your lifestyle, occupation, and health profile.
